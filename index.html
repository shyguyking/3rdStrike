So what is Street Fighter III: Third Strike? In one simple phrase it can be described as limitless possibilities. Third strike is the final installment in the Street Fighter III series of games in which you fight one on one against an opponent. This fighting game is full of fast-paced action where split second decisions can lead to either a great victory or a crushing defeat. But, why play third strike when there are so many other options to choose from in the genre? This site should help provide a glimpse into what makes third strike the masterpiece that it is via its many intricacies and idiosyncracies that set it apart from the competition.

Unique Gameplay

The gameplay of third strike is unparalleled in all of the fighting game genre. It serves to revolutionize what can and can not be done in a one on one fighter due to the complexity of the characters, mechanics, and systems set in place by the game. All of these seemingly simple mechanics at face value combine together to form the advanced metagame that is third strike. No one option in this game has any set respone. The sky is the limit when it comes to the myriads of situations that the game may put the player into at a split-seconds notice. This variability in gameplay is what sets third strike's inherent gameplay apart from the rest.

Superb Artstyle

What can be said about the art of third strike? It has often been called the pinnacle of sprite based artwork in the fighting game genre. Street Fighter III: Third Strike is used as the golden standard as to what a two-dimensional fighting game looks like. This can be seen in the vibance of the colors down to each individual frame of the animations of the characters ebbing and flowing to create a seemless visual experience.

Evo Moment 37


What can be said about Evo Moment 37 that has not already been said? This moment is the quintessential moment in Western Fighting Game History and the clip is regarded as the most highly viewed clip in fighting game community history. With several books written about the momentous occasion itself, the significance of the moment and the game itself is the reason that fighting games still exist in the west. Street Fighter and other two-dimensional fighting games as we know them today likely would not exist had it not been for this display of mastery of third strike. This moment alone encapsulates the love and dedication that third strike fans around the world have put in to this game that they love so dearly.
